What is the Coupe du Monde de la Pâtisserie (World Pastry cup)? +

The Coupe du Monde de la Pâtisserie (World Pastry Cup) is the most prestigious pastry competition in the world. It was founded in 1989 by the legendary French pastry chef Gabriel Paillasson, who envisioned a global stage to showcase the technical mastery and artistic excellence of the pastry profession.

As Paillasson once said: It was a magnificent event with an amazing atmosphere that reflects pastry's evolution in the world.

The event operates on a bi-annual cycle (every two years). To reach the Grand Final in Lyon, France, teams must navigate a rigorous selection process, nationals, continentals and then the Grand Final.

Candidates must typically have at least 5 years of professional experience. Each country must have a recognized national club to enter the competition.

A standard team consists of a Chocolatier, a Sugar Artist, a Frozen Dessert Specialist, and a Team Leader / President.

Each edition has a mandatory theme. For example, the 2021 theme was All Art is an Imitation of Nature.

The highest honor is the Gold Medal (World Champion Title), the 1st Place. But they also give trophies for the best of each discipline: Chocolate Prize, Sugar Prize, Frozen Dessert / Ice Prize and the Vase de Sèvres.

Mexico's path to the World Cup +

Mexico’s journey in the Coupe du Monde de la Pâtisserie has been one of steady evolution since its 1995 debut. After years of placing between 11th and 17th, the national team achieved a historic milestone by breaking into the global elite. Avoranti proudly celebrates the team’s mastery and discipline in securing 8th place, cementing Mexico’s position among the world’s top ten pastry talents.

The selection process for the 2021 edition was filled with challenges:

Copa Vatel México (2019): The national selection took place during the Sirha Mexico event, where the best local talents competed for the honor of representing the flag.

Americas Cup (2020) - Cancelled: Due to the global COVID-19 pandemic, the continental selection in Chile was cancelled. As a result, the teams were selected based on their previous rankings and dossiers to ensure the competition could move forward.
