What is the Coupe du Monde de la Pâtisserie (World Pastry cup)?

The Coupe du Monde de la Pâtisserie (World Pastry Cup) is the most prestigious pastry competition in the world. It was founded in 1989 by the legendary French pastry chef Gabriel Paillasson, who envisioned a global stage to showcase the technical mastery and artistic excellence of the pastry profession.

As Paillasson once said:

"It was a magnificent event with an amazing atmosphere that reflects pastry's evolution in the world."
The Competition Cycle

The event operates on a bi-annual cycle (every two years). To reach the Grand Final in Lyon, France, teams must navigate a rigorous selection process:

  • National Selections: Over 50 countries hold internal competitions to find their best talent.
  • Continental Selections: Qualifying teams move to continental rounds (Americas, Europe, Asia-Pacific, Africa, and Middle East) to win their spot in the final.
  • Grand Final: The top 20 countries face off in a live battle at the Sirha trade show in Lyon. Each team has exactly 10 hours to complete all their tests. The live event lasts 2 days.
Team Composition and Roles

Candidates must typically have at least 5 years of professional experience. Each country must have a recognized national club to enter the competition.

A standard team consists of:

  • Chocolatier: Specialized in artistic chocolate sculptures and candies.
  • Sugar Artist: Expert in pulled, blown, and cast sugar showpieces.
  • Frozen Dessert Specialist: Focused on ice sculpting and frozen entremets.
  • Team Leader / President: Usually a renowned chef who guides the team and serves as a jury member (grading other teams, but never their own).

Categories & Themes
  • Categories: Teams must create a series of tasting desserts (iced fruit desserts, restaurant-style desserts) and spectacular artistic sculptures in chocolate, sugar, and sculpted ice.
  • Themes: Each edition has a mandatory theme. For example, the 2021 theme was All Art is an Imitation of Nature
Individual and Technical Special Prizes

The highest honor is the Gold Medal (World Champion Title) 1st Place.

They also give trophies for the best of each disipline:

  • Chocolate Prize: Awarded for the most exceptional chocolate sculpture and tasting quality.
  • Sugar Prize: Given to the expert who demonstrates the best technique in pulled, blown, and cast sugar.
  • Frozen Dessert / Ice Prize: Recognizes mastery in ice carving and frozen entremets.
  • Vase de Sèvres: A porcelain trophy donated by the President of the French Republic, awarded to the team with the highest score in "Buffet Presentation."

Mexico's path to the World Cup

Mexico's participation in the Coupe du Monde de la Pâtisserie is a story of constant growth. The country began its journey in this prestigious competition in 1995, marking the first time Mexican talent was showcased on the world stage in Lyon.

Before the historic 2021 results, the Mexican national team had never broken into the Top 10 of the Grand Finale, previously consistently placing between 11th and 17th place in the world. Celebrating the Top 10 Milestone
